She Is Like the Moon

She is like the moon—not loud, not chasing light, yet somehow, she brightens my night.

She doesn't try to be seen, but heaven makes her glow. Not because she wants attention, but because she reflects the One I know.

"The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it." —John 1:5

She is like the moon—there is calm in her presence, like a prayer I didn't speak but God still answered.

"Peace I leave with you; my peace I give you. I do not give to you as the world gives. Do not let your hearts be troubled and do not be afraid." —John 14:27

She is like the moon—sometimes hidden by clouds, but always there. Even in silence, she reminds me that faith shines quietly.

"Then Jesus told him, 'Because you have seen me, you have believed; blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed." —John 20:29

She is like the moon—never asking for praise, yet teaching me grace. She is not the sun, but she leads me to it.

"He must become greater; I must become less." —John 3:30

And if you ask me why I admire her—it’s simple:

Because when I look at her, I see a quiet kind of miracle.

Not mine to own, but mine to thank God for.

Because she is like the moon—sent not to stay, but to remind me that even in the dark, God still gives light.
