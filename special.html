She Is Like the Moon

She is like the moon—
Not loud, not chasing light,
Yet somehow, she brightens my night.
She doesn't try to be seen, but heaven makes her glow.

Not because she wants attention,
But because she reflects the One I know.
A quiet beam in the chaos,
A gentle echo of holy flow.

"The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it." — John 1:5

She is like the moon—
There is calm in her presence,
Like a prayer I didn't speak
But God still answered with her essence.

"Peace I leave with you; my peace I give you. I do not give to you as the world gives. Do not let your hearts be troubled and do not be afraid." — John 14:27

She is like the moon—
Sometimes hidden by clouds,
But always there in the hush,
A steady light beneath the shroud.

Even in silence, she reminds me
That faith shines quietly.
Her presence is not loud,
But it is holy, and it is free.

"Then Jesus told him, 'Because you have seen me, you have believed; blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed.'" — John 20:29

She is like the moon—
Never asking for praise,
Yet teaching me grace
In her steady, selfless ways.

She is not the sun,
But she leads me to it.
A reflection of love,
So soft, yet true to it.

"He must become greater; I must become less." — John 3:30

And if you ask me why I admire her—
It’s simple and pure, not grand or blur.
Because when I look at her,
I see a quiet kind of miracle.

Not mine to own,
But mine to thank God for.
She is like the moon—
Sent not to stay, but to remind me:

Even in the dark,
God still gives light.
And some miracles
Shine best at night.

Like a Tulip in Bloom

She is like a tulip—graceful in the light,
Not seeking the spotlight, yet glowing bright.
Her presence speaks without a word,
A soft bloom where love is heard.

"She is clothed with strength and dignity; she can laugh at the days to come."
—Proverbs 31:25

She is like a tulip—standing strong and tall,
Rooted deep in faith, no fear to fall.
In storms and winds, she stays in place,
A quiet power, a steady grace.

"The Lord is my strength and my shield; my heart trusts in him, and he helps me."
—Psalm 28:7

She is like a tulip—vibrant with hope,
A living prayer, a way to cope.
Her colors speak of healing’s touch,
A silent message, a love so much.

"He heals the brokenhearted and binds up their wounds."
—Psalm 147:3

She is like a tulip—gentle in her way,
Her beauty speaks without words to say.
Not loud, but deep, she shines within,
A heart of grace where love begins.

"Your beauty should not come from outward adornment… rather, it should be that of your inner self…"
—1 Peter 3:3-4

She is like a tulip—blooming where she’s placed,
Each moment, each season, with steady grace.
Not to be owned, but to be seen,
A reminder that love's always serene.

"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here!"
—2 Corinthians 5:17

And if you ask why I admire her—it’s plain:
In her, I see God's grace in gentle reign.
Not for my grasp, but for His light to pour,
For she is like a tulip—blooming forevermore.

"The Lord will fulfill his purpose for me; your love, O Lord, endures forever—do not abandon the works of your hands."
—Psalm 138:8

And as I look ahead with hopeful eyes,
I wait for the tulip that God will rise—
Not by my will, but by His design,
A bloom in His timing, forever divine.

"For I know the plans I have for you,” declares the Lord, “plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you a hope and a future."
—Jeremiah 29:11

She Is Like a Swan

Have you seen a swan upon the lake,
So calm above, though waters shake?
A silent strength, a quiet song—
She’s never rushed, yet moves along.

Her beauty’s not in what she wears,
But in the way she lifts her prayers.
And though this world may race and run,
She glides beneath the morning sun.

“He leads me beside still waters; He restores my soul.”
— Psalm 23:2–3

She doesn't shout to make her mark,
Yet leaves a glow within the dark.
Her words are few, but when they fall—
They heal, they build, they carry all.

And I confess with soul undone—
That’s why I admire her—the swan.
She walks in grace, untouched by flame,
She doesn’t strive, yet hearts proclaim.

“In returning and rest you shall be saved; in quietness and trust shall be your strength.”
— Isaiah 30:15

They say swans love with steady fire,
A vow that time cannot expire.
So does she—so still, so true,
She loves like rain, not like the dew.

She does not leave when seasons shift,
She stays, she prays, she learns to lift.
And in a world that loves to run—
I pause, because she does not shun.

And oh—that’s why I admire her still,
Her quiet strength, her steady will.

“The integrity of the upright guides them, but the crookedness of the treacherous destroys them.”
— Proverbs 11:3

I wonder—how her heart stays bright
In waters filled with war and night.
While others fight to just be seen,
She floats untouched, yet deep, serene.

Perhaps it’s not the waves she braves,
But the One who walks upon the waves.
Her grace is not her own to show—
It's borrowed light. It's Spirit’s flow.

“Blessed are the pure in heart, for they shall see God.”
— Matthew 5:8

She doesn't chase the crowns of men,
But wears a peace beyond their ken.
No thrones, no stage, no prideful dawn—
Just stillness wrapped in wings withdrawn.

And that is why I stand in awe,
At how she lives without a flaw.
She floats in faith, through dusk and dawn—
I deeply admire her.
She is the swan.

“But they who wait for the Lord shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings like eagles…”
— Isaiah 40:31

So I pray—
Lord, let me never take her for granted,
Let me honor her heart, so candid.
And if I am to love her true,
Let it be with the love You give too.

For she is the swan I prayed for,
And in her, I see Your love, once more.

The White Heart I Admire

Have you seen a soul so soft, so still,
Like morning light upon a hill?
Not loud, not proud, not chasing fame,
Yet every step—He knows her name.

She walks not high, but walks with grace,
A holy hush upon her face.
She doesn’t boast, she doesn’t shine,
Yet somehow makes the heavens align.

“Let your garments always be white, and let your head lack no oil.”
— Ecclesiastes 9:8

How can her heart remain so pure,
In a world so fast, so unsure?
She doesn’t scream, she doesn’t fight,
Yet darkness bows before her light.

She is not perfect, yet she’s rare,
A breath of calm, a silent prayer.
She doesn't rule, yet she inspires—
A quiet spark that sets wild fires.

“You shall be blameless before the Lord your God.”
— Deuteronomy 18:13

Have you heard the snow when it starts to fall?
How softly it covers, how it silences all?
That's how her presence enters the room—
Gentle as light, yet breaking the gloom.

She forgives before she’s asked,
Lays down her pride, removes the mask.
She doesn’t act to be admired—
But leaves you still, your soul rewired.

“Who can say, ‘I have made my heart clean, I am pure from my sin’?”
— Proverbs 20:9

She doesn’t wear a crown of gold,
But wears a truth that’s centuries old.
She clings to Christ, she walks His way,
And lets His mercy guide her day.

So tell me now, how could I not
Admire the soul the world forgot?
The one who walks in holy white,
Whose spirit sings in silent light?

“The Lord knows those who are His, and let everyone who names the name of Christ depart from iniquity.”
— 2 Timothy 2:19

She’s not just kind—she’s set apart,
With heaven etched upon her heart.
And though she never seeks the throne,
She leads by love, and not alone.

She is the white heart I revere—
So pure, so strong, so crystal clear.
And though her favorite color is white,
It’s her soul that truly shines that light.

What Eyes Don’t See

How does she shine in a world so loud,
Where dreams dissolve and hope wears out?
While chaos carves through every street,
She walks in grace, with steady feet.

She isn’t moved by shifting sand,
Her heart is held in higher hands.
She walks by truth the world denies—
She sees through Heaven’s silent eyes.

And I wonder, what’s her secret light?
What lifts her soul through darkest nights?
She holds a verse, not just in speech—
But in her bones, her soul, her reach.

"No eye has seen," the verse begins,
"No ear has heard," where hope begins.
"No mind has known what God’s prepared
For those who love Him, those who dare."

That’s her anthem, that’s her core—
When life is less, she trusts there’s more.
She lives like promise is already done,
Like storms just pass before the sun.

And in her quiet, I see strength—
Not in noise, but in the length
Of every trial she’s overcome,
With faith that speaks when words are none.

I’ve watched her love when love is rare,
Give when empty, stand in prayer.
Not chasing crowns the world can give,
But treasures found in how she lives.

And maybe that's what draws me near—
Not just her strength, but how she hears
A voice beyond what earth can show—
A faith in things I long to know.

She is the verse she holds inside,
A living proof that God still guides.
And in a world that cannot see,
She walks with faith—and that moves me.
