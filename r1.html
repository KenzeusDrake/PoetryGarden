Throttle Therapy: A Moment of Reflection

I remember those days—random, yet clear,
Throttle therapy, a moment of fear.
Rushing, racing, the engine’s roar,
Chasing a high that left me wanting more.

It felt like freedom, but in truth, a cage,
A fleeting escape, yet bound by rage.
The speed, the noise, the endless chase,
I thought it would fill that empty space.

But now, in the stillness, I see the cost—
A part of myself I almost lost.
Chasing that rush, I lost my way,
Forgot who I was, who I am today.

I don’t want to go back to that wasted time,
The life I lived was a mountain to climb.
Each rev of the engine drowned my soul,
I thought I was whole, but never felt whole.

Now, I stand here, grounded and free,
No more chaos to define me.
I remember the throttle, but I chose a different course,
One that heals, one that draws from a greater source.

In my past, there was pain and strife,
But I’ve found peace beyond that life.
Throttle therapy may have brought me near,
But now I know—my true path is clear.
