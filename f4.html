In the Noise, I Hear You Still

Why is it loud when my heart wants quiet?
Why do they laugh while my soul stays silent?
Cards are shuffling, bingo's called—
But why does joy feel so forestalled?

Can’t they hear the ache I hide?
The pain that echoes deep inside?
A singer sings, a mournful hymn—
But why do my eyes still feel so dim?

Lolo… do you hear me now?
Do you see me standing, asking how
The world kept turning when you’re gone—
When every second feels too long?

Remember when I made a fuss,
Too noisy, wild, rebellious, thus—
You’d twist my ear and shake your head,
"Be still," you’d say, and off I fled.

But now… who twists my ear today?
Who pulls me back when I’ve gone astray?
Who tells me "hush" when I get loud?
Who anchors me when I'm too proud?

You said, “Just lay me in our home,
No need for marble, grandiose stone.”
And I respect it, I do, I swear—
But why does peace feel so unfair?

I wanted roses, gold-lined gates,
A thousand lights, poetic fates.
But you—
You wanted only rest
In the house where you felt loved the best.

So now you stay, within these walls,
Where every echo gently calls,
Where slippers once would scuff the floor—
But you, dear Lolo, walk no more.

And still I ask:
Will I forget your voice in time?
Will silence steal what once was mine?
No—
Because in noise, I hear you still,
In every scold, in every thrill.

And when I laugh too loud someday,
I’ll look above, and smile and say:
“I know, Lolo—too loud again.”
And miss your pinch that felt like rain.

So sleep in peace, but stay nearby.
Don’t let these memories say goodbye.
For in my heart, your voice will stay—
Forever loud in your quiet way.
