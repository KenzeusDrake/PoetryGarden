When God Sends Little Lights

Some days feel like walking through a fog,
Heavy with unspoken struggles,
And quiet battles no one sees—
I've been carrying the weight.

Wondering if anyone notices
How hard it's been, how lost I feel
In the silence, in the ache,
In the waiting that won't heal.

Then today—in the middle of all that weight—
A message came. From Mom. A screenshot.
An appreciation post from someone I'd helped—
Someone who's grown, someone I never imagined.

She said, "Thank you, kuya."
And for a moment, my heart smiled.
Even just for a moment, God reminded me:
You are seen. You are valued.

You are not alone.
He didn't take the pain away—
But He sent a spark of joy
To carry me through it. Because that's who He is.

The God who finds a way
To reach us right when we need it most.
With whispers, with moments,
With little lights that bring us home.

"The Lord is close to the brokenhearted, and saves those who are crushed in spirit."
— Psalm 34:18

The Blooming Ache

The sakura is blooming again—
Soft, fragile, and full of life.
But here I am, standing under its petals,
Carrying a heart that's missing someone.

It's strange, how life keeps going
Even when a piece of you
Feels stuck in yesterday—
A moment you can't outgrow.

I smile. I move. I show up.
But inside, there's a silent ache,
Blooming with every reminder—
A memory. A voice. A familiar place.

And suddenly, I’m back to that moment—
When you were here,
And then, you weren’t.
A soft goodbye I never chose.

But maybe that's what love does.
It lingers. It aches. It stays.
And it keeps blooming,
Even when the season has changed.

So I let the ache bloom—
Not to drown in it,
But to honor you,
And the love that still remains.

Because love like that never dies—
It just becomes a softer kind of presence.
A quiet comfort in the petals,
A whispered “I miss you” on the breeze.

"Blessed are those who mourn, for they will be comforted."
— Matthew 5:4

In the Heart of Forever

Obasan, though you're no longer here,
Your love still echoes, crystal clear.
The whispers of your voice remain,
A song that heals, a soft refrain.

Where are you now, in heaven's light?
Do you feel my tears in the quiet of the night?
I miss your laugh, your gentle smile,
The way you'd hold me for a while.

Your wisdom like a river’s flow,
Still nourishes my heart and soul.
Why did you have to go so soon?
Could I have had just one more afternoon?

But in my heart, you live and stay,
Guiding me in every way.
Do you watch me as I grow and strive,
Encouraging me to keep alive?

Though I wish you were still here,
I trust God's plan, I have no fear.
You’ve taught me love, you've shown me grace,
Your lessons etched in time and space.

Obasan, you’re with angels now,
Though I long for you, I know somehow,
It’s His will that calls you near,
And for that, my heart feels clear.

Do you see how far I’ve come,
How every step still leads me home?
Your spirit lives in all I do,
In every prayer, I carry you.

I wanted you back, to hold your hand,
But I trust in God's divine command.
I thank the Lord for all you gave,
And for the love you taught me to save.

Obasan, you'll always be my guide,
In my heart, you will reside.
No time or place can change this fact,
Your love is here, and that’s a pact.

And though you’ve passed beyond this earth,
In my heart, you know your worth.
Forever cherished, forever dear,
Obasan, you’ll always be near.

In the Noise, I Hear You Still

Why is it loud when my heart wants quiet?
Why do they laugh while my soul stays silent?
Cards are shuffling, bingo's called—
But why does joy feel so forestalled?

Can’t they hear the ache I hide?
The pain that echoes deep inside?
A singer sings, a mournful hymn—
But why do my eyes still feel so dim?

Lolo… do you hear me now?
Do you see me standing, asking how
The world kept turning when you’re gone—
When every second feels too long?

Remember when I made a fuss,
Too noisy, wild, rebellious, thus—
You’d twist my ear and shake your head,
"Be still," you’d say, and off I fled.

But now… who twists my ear today?
Who pulls me back when I’ve gone astray?
Who tells me "hush" when I get loud?
Who anchors me when I'm too proud?

You said, “Just lay me in our home,
No need for marble, grandiose stone.”
And I respect it, I do, I swear—
But why does peace feel so unfair?

I wanted roses, gold-lined gates,
A thousand lights, poetic fates.
But you—
You wanted only rest
In the house where you felt loved the best.

So now you stay, within these walls,
Where every echo gently calls,
Where slippers once would scuff the floor—
But you, dear Lolo, walk no more.

And still I ask:
Will I forget your voice in time?
Will silence steal what once was mine?
No—
Because in noise, I hear you still,
In every scold, in every thrill.

And when I laugh too loud someday,
I’ll look above, and smile and say:
“I know, Lolo—too loud again.”
And miss your pinch that felt like rain.

So sleep in peace, but stay nearby.
Don’t let these memories say goodbye.
For in my heart, your voice will stay—
Forever loud in your quiet way.
