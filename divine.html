Boundless Grace, Broken Chains

(Inspired by "O Come to the Altar")

Are you tired? Tired of running,
Tired of hiding, tired of pretending you're okay?
Does your soul feel heavy,
Like the weight of everything you've done is too much to carry?

Listen, there's a place where your burdens can rest,
A place where the weight of your past doesn't define your future.
It's not in your own strength,
But in the open arms of grace.

"O come to the altar," He calls,
Not with judgment, but with invitation—
A Father's voice, asking you to come just as you are,
Broken and weary, and let His love make you whole.

The cross wasn't just a sacrifice—
It was a declaration of freedom.
Nailed hands, open arms, saying,
"Come, and lay down your chains."

No matter the scars,
No matter the shame—
There is freedom in His name.
Boundless grace. Broken chains.

These aren't just words; they are a promise—
A promise that your story doesn't end in defeat,
But in redemption.
Why wait?

Why carry the weight
When He already carried it for you?
Why live in fear
When He has already overcome the grave?

The altar is open—
Not a place of condemnation,
But a place of healing.
Come—drop your pain, drop your doubt, and let grace rewrite your story.

Boundless grace. Broken chains.
The invitation is simple:
Come to the altar,
Where love still bleeds for you, where you are made new,
Where your chains fall, and your soul is free.

The Ache That Prayer Holds

I still can't sleep tonight.
My eyes are wide, but my heart is tired.
I keep staring at the ceiling,
Hoping it would give me answers—answers to a question I never wanted to ask:

Why so soon? Why you? Why now?
Everything happened so fast.
One moment, you were here—breathing, laughing, living.
The next, you were a memory I wasn't ready to keep.

A silence I never wanted to hear.
I still replay your voice in my mind,
Hold on to the echo of your laughter,
As if remembering you could somehow bring you back.

But it doesn't. And that truth crushes me
Over and over again.
It doesn't feel real. Some days, I wait
For a call that will never come.

I hold back tears in crowded rooms,
Then weep alone when the world goes quiet.
Yet in this unbearable ache, I stand—
Shaking, grieving, questioning—but still standing.

Because I believe in the God who never makes mistakes,
Even when His ways feel like shattered glass beneath my feet.
I trust Him, even when my heart screams in confusion.
I believe, even when I don't understand.

And I hold on, even when letting go feels impossible.
Because I know—one day, this pain will bloom into peace.
And somehow, it will be well...
Not because I feel it now, but because He said so.

"He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain, for the old order of things has passed away."
— Revelation 21:4

Faith That Lives Louder Than Words

(Inspired by Titus)

Ever wonder why your faith still feels?
Like roots that thirst but never fill?
Could it be it's not just what you believe,
But the life you live, the grace you give?

What if I told you, this letter—so brief,
From Paul to Titus, brings heart to belief?
A church with promise, but lost in the storm,
With chaos, false teaching, and no true form.

Enter Titus—not green, not unsure,
But steadfast and faithful, grounded and pure.
He didn't just teach, he became the way.
Living the truth in the light of day.

Paul didn't just send a scroll to recite,
He said, "Let them see shine with the light."
Because doctrine alone can't carry the weight
If your hands don't move and your love comes late.

"He must hold firmly to the trustworthy message as it has been taught, so that he can encourage others by sound doctrine and refute those who oppose it." (Titus 1:9, NIV)

That's kind of reliable. Not just vocal, but credible.
Visible. Viable.

When I was younger, I thought faith was knowing.
That learning the Word meant I was growing.
But faith isn't built on verses alone—
It's seen in the quiet, when you're all on your own.

It's kindness given with no one to praise,
It's showing up strong on the hardest of days.
It's grace that forgives when the hurt runs deep,
It's promises made that your soul will keep.

Let's be honest—faith that speaks but doesn't act,
Feels hollow, like words that truth has lacked.
You can preach all you want, raise your voice to the sky.
But if your life lies silent, people pass it by.

Titus reminds us: grace is not still,
It's a fire that shapes both heart and will.
So don't cling to the past with a "that was me" line—
Because grace transforms—leaves the old behind.

So here's the question, loud and clear:
When they look at your life, what do they hear?
Do they see real faith, humble and true?
Or just another routine the church folk do?

Let's not just talk—let's let it out,
Live the faith we preach, remove all doubt.
For faith that shows is the faith that stays,
And shines like light in the darkest of days.

When Favor Finds You

(Inspired by Esther 4:14, Romans 8:28, and Proverbs 3:5–6)

I didn’t plan this path I tread,
Nor dreamed these doors where I’d be led.
I lived a life so still, unseen,
Content in quiet — calm and clean.

But then He moved—without a sound,
And favor found me, holy ground.
No signs, no maps, no curtain drawn,
Just grace that led my feet at dawn.

“Trust in the Lord with all your heart,
and lean not on your own insight.
In all your ways acknowledge Him,
and He will make your pathways right.”
— Proverbs 3:5–6

At first, I thought, “Oh, this is mine!”
A gift of heaven’s grand design.
But time revealed a deeper view:
This blessing wasn’t just for you.

It flowed to me, but not to stay,
It’s meant to shape, to light the way.
Like Esther crowned, yet not for show,
She rose so others too might grow.

“And who knows but that you have come
to royal place for such a time?”
— Esther 4:14, its silent drum
Now beats within this heart of mine.

It wasn’t skill that brought me near,
Nor strength that overcame my fear.
It was obedience, clothed in grace,
A trembling “yes” in hidden place.

Through silent prayers and waiting still,
Through letting go of lesser will,
I found that favor does not chase
The proud, but meets the lowly place.

“And we know that in all things God
Works for the good, both wide and broad,
For those who love Him, walk His way—
He weaves the night into the day.”
— Romans 8:28

So if you’re standing where I stood,
Unsure if this is bad or good,
Remember this: God sees it all—
He writes His glory through your call.

It’s not just to you blessings flow,
But through you, others come to know
That favor's not for pride or show—
It’s how His love and truth will grow.

So take the step, though small, unsure,
Your trembling faith, He will secure.
And when you stand in heaven’s view,
You’ll see — God’s favor carried through.

The Rubik’s Cube of Life

Life is like a Rubik’s Cube—
Twisted, turned, and sometimes crude.
The colors spin, the sides collide,
Each move I make, a step I slide.

"For everything there is a season, a time for every activity under heaven."
—Ecclesiastes 3:1

At times, it feels like all is lost,
A tangled mess, no matter the cost.
I twist and turn, but nothing fits,
A puzzle broken into bits.

But in the chaos, I find a truth—
Even the mess has its own proof.
Each turn, each twist, each move I make,
Shapes the path that I must take.

"And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose."
—Romans 8:28

Some pieces don't align just right,
But with patience, I see the light.
Through every wrong, a lesson learned,
In every loss, there’s something earned.

"Not only so, but we also glory in our sufferings, because we know that suffering produces perseverance; perseverance, character; and character, hope."
—Romans 5:3-4

The cube of life may twist again,
But through the struggle, I find my zen.
For though it's hard to see the way,
In time, the pieces will obey.

"The Lord will fulfill his purpose for me; your love, O Lord, endures forever—do not abandon the works of your hands."
—Psalm 138:8

It’s never perfect, this game I play,
But I find the pieces day by day.
Life may be a Rubik’s Cube, a test,
But I’ll fix myself—putting my soul to rest.

"I can do all things through Christ who gives me strength."
—Philippians 4:13

Each twist, each turn, a chance to grow,
To heal, to change, to let love flow.
For in this cube, I see the grace—
That even in chaos, I find my place.

No Turning Back: Manatiling Liwanag sa Dilim

I once was lost, a soul adrift,
Caught in the wind, no steady lift.
I walked in doubt, I walked in fear,
My faith was small, my heart unclear.

I searched for answers on my own,
Through paths of pain, through seeds once sown.
But all I found were broken dreams,
A heart that cracked and lost its seams.

But then, a light broke through the dark,
A whisper called, igniting spark.
A hand that reached, a voice so kind,
Guided my heart, redefined.

From unbelief, I learned to stand,
A life once lost, now in His hand.
The roots of faith began to grow,
And I was not the one to sow.

For now, my faith is like a tree,
Planted firm in what I can't see.
Its roots run deep beneath the ground,
In the Lord’s love, I’ve found my sound.

No more a soul tossed by the storm,
For now I stand, my heart reborn.
Faith like a mountain, strong and tall,
I will not crumble, I will not fall.

I trust the path, I trust the way,
No matter what the world may say.
For His plans are greater than mine,
I walk by faith, His will divine.

I once was weak, but now I’m strong,
In Him, I find where I belong.
Faith is my fortress, faith is my song,
And with His grace, I will go long.

No turning back, no second guess,
For my faith now is firm, no less.
It’s anchored deep, it’s built to last,
Rooted in truth, unshaken fast.

I stand today, I rise again,
In every storm, through every pain.
My faith is stronger, I won’t sway—
For with Proverbs 3:5-6, I’ll find my way.

More Doesn’t Always Mean Worth

It was Easter Sunday.
My little sister looked up at me with bright eyes.
“Kuya, can we do the egg hunt? Just like before… with obasan?”
Her words brought me back to days filled with obasan’s laughter,
when joy echoed through the house like music.
I smiled.
“Ay yah, tara. Game.”

Together, we prepared the eggs—16 in total:
5 eggs with ₱500
5 eggs with ₱100
5 eggs with ₱200
1 special egg with ₱1,000

We carefully tucked them away—
behind flower pots, under benches, inside slippers, beneath leaves—
laughing as we tried to be sneaky.
The kids waited eagerly inside the house, peeking through windows.
When the last egg was hidden, I shouted, “Okay, ready na!”

And off they ran.

Kenji, 8, found 3 eggs.
Alyssa, clever and quick, found 4.
Jomar, 17, snatched 7.
Two cousins found the last 2.

After the search, they gathered, out of breath but grinning.
“Kuya, ang dami mong nakuha! Sana all!” Alyssa teased.
Jomar laughed, “Mabilis kasi ako maghanap!”

But when we opened the eggs, the results surprised everyone.

Kenji, with 3 eggs, had ₱1,700
Alyssa, with 4 eggs, also had ₱1,700
Jomar, with 7 eggs, had ₱1,300
The cousins got ₱100 and ₱200

Jomar stared, confused.
He thought he’d won because he had the most.
But it wasn’t about how many—
it was about what was inside.

I smiled and shared the lesson:
“Sa buhay, hindi palaging mas marami ang may pinakamataas na halaga.
Sometimes, the ones with the most don’t always have the most value.
It’s not about how much you have,
but about the heart behind it.”

The Widow’s Offering (Mark 12:41–44)

Jesus was sitting near the temple treasury, watching people give their offerings.
The rich gave large sums, but a poor widow came forward,
giving only two small coins—less than a penny.

Jesus said to His disciples:
“Truly I tell you, this poor widow has put more into the treasury than all the others. They all gave out of their wealth; but she, out of her poverty, put in everything—all she had to live on.”

Her offering wasn’t large,
but it was great in heart.
She gave all she had,
and in God’s eyes, that was more valuable than the rich gifts of many others.

David’s Anointing (1 Samuel 16:7)

When Samuel went to anoint the next king,
he saw Jesse’s sons—strong, tall, and impressive.
But God spoke to him and said:

“Do not consider his appearance or his height, for I have rejected him. The Lord does not look at the things people look at. People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart.”

God chose David, the youngest, a shepherd—
the one no one thought of.
God saw his heart, his faithfulness, and his love for God.
Outward appearances don’t matter to God; it’s the heart that counts.

The Lesson:

In life, it’s not about how much we have,
but what we carry inside.
Not the size of our offering, but the spirit in which we give it.
Not how we appear, but who we are at the core.

Just like the egg hunt:
Those with fewer eggs received more in value.
It’s not always about quantity.
It’s about quality. Intent. Love.

And those two cousins—
they didn’t get the biggest prizes,
but they smiled just the same.
They weren’t chasing the money.
They were chasing the moment.

And I whispered, “I remember of Obasan…”
I held the eggs, not for the cash inside,
but for the memory wrapped in plastic shells.
The joy of the search.
The laughter of family.
The feeling of being together.

Sometimes, the greatest prize isn't in the egg—
but in the moment you shared looking for it.

Mark 10:31 (NIV):
“But many who are first will be last, and the last first.”

In God’s eyes,
those who seem to have the least
often hold the richest treasures.

So, in the end,
it’s not about how many eggs you find,
or how much you gain.
It’s about what you do with what you have,
and whether you offer it with a heart that loves, remembers, and trusts in God.

“…but the Lord looks at the heart” (1 Samuel 16:7)

When the Tomb Was Still Cold, but My Faith Was Already Alive

I didn’t expect 2k25 to feel like this.
Not with lilies.
Not with light.
But with loss.

Two consecutive days of April—
my dad went home, my lolo followed.
Back-to-back grief.
Like Good Friday never ended.

And yet, somehow…
My faith never wavered.
Not this time.

Because I remember when I used to doubt.
When my obasan passed away,
I didn’t just bury her—
I buried my faith with her.

I let the silence convince me that God was absent.
I lived like someone forgotten,
Breathing, but barely believing.

Until someone came into my life—
Not with preaching, but presence.
Not with judgment, but gentleness.
They walked beside me,
And through them, God whispered:

“I never left you.”

And slowly, I came back.
Broken, but believing.
Wounded, but worshiping.
And now, I can say with every tear:
I trust You, Lord.
Not just in blessing—
But also in the breaking.

Today, I didn’t cling to flowers.
I clung to the Seven Last Words of Jesus.
And through each one, He brought me peace in my pain.

1. “Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they do.” – Luke 23:34 (NKJV)

Jesus said, “Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they are doing.” — Luke 23:34 (NIV)

And I wept.
Because I was once the one who didn’t know.
Didn’t know how deeply He loved me.
Didn’t know how much I needed forgiveness.

But He forgave me before I asked.
And now, I live in that mercy.

“Forgive as the Lord forgave you.” — Colossians 3:13 (NIV)

2. “Assuredly, I say to you, today you will be with Me in Paradise.” – Luke 23:43 (NKJV)

Jesus answered him, “Truly I tell you, today you will be with me in paradise.” — Luke 23:43 (NIV)

And I held this tightly.
Because my dad—
My lolo—
They knew Jesus.
And Jesus promised today, not someday.

So I don’t grieve like one without hope.

“My Father’s house has many rooms… I am going there to prepare a place for you… I will come back and take you to be with me that you also may be where I am.” — John 14:2–3 (NIV)

3. “Woman, behold your son!” … “Behold your mother!” – John 19:26–27 (NKJV)

When Jesus saw his mother there, and the disciple whom he loved standing nearby, he said to her, “Woman, here is your son,” and to the disciple, “Here is your mother.” — John 19:26–27 (NIV)

Even in dying, Jesus formed family.
And now, I look at who’s left.
We are scarred—
But still standing.
And that means we carry each other now.

“Carry each other’s burdens, and in this way you will fulfill the law of Christ.” — Galatians 6:2 (NIV)

4. “My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?” – Matthew 27:46 (NKJV)

About three in the afternoon Jesus cried out in a loud voice… “My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?” — Matthew 27:46 (NIV)

I used to feel forsaken.
But now I know—
If Jesus cried those words,
Then even my darkest cries are still prayers.

“The Lord is close to the brokenhearted and saves those who are crushed in spirit.” — Psalm 34:18 (NIV)

“So do not fear, for I am with you… I will strengthen you and help you; I will uphold you with my righteous right hand.” — Isaiah 41:10 (NIV)

5. “I thirst!” – John 19:28 (NKJV)

Later, knowing that everything had now been finished, and so that Scripture would be fulfilled, Jesus said, “I am thirsty.” — John 19:28 (NIV)

I thirsted once.
For peace.
For purpose.
For someone to say it’ll be okay.

But Jesus quenched my soul with Living Water.

“Whoever drinks the water I give them will never thirst. Indeed, the water I give them will become in them a spring of water welling up to eternal life.” — John 4:14 (NIV)

6. “It is finished!” – John 19:30 (NKJV)

When he had received the drink, Jesus said, “It is finished.” With that, he bowed his head and gave up his spirit. — John 19:30 (NIV)

It didn’t feel finished when I lost them.
It felt like everything ended.
But Jesus didn’t say, “I am finished”—
He said, “It is.”
The battle. The curse. The grave.

“I consider that our present sufferings are not worth comparing with the glory that will be revealed in us.” — Romans 8:18 (NIV)

7. “Father, into Your hands I commit My spirit.” – Luke 23:46 (NKJV)

Jesus called out with a loud voice, “Father, into your hands I commit my spirit.” When he had said this, he breathed his last. — Luke 23:46 (NIV)

And now—so do I.
Not with fear,
But with faith.
Not because I understand the plan,
But because I trust the One who holds it.

“God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble.” — Psalm 46:1 (NIV)

“Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight.” — Proverbs 3:5–6 (NIV)

So here I am.
Still grieving.
But grounded.
Still weeping.
But worshiping.
Still missing them.
But never again missing God.

Because someone helped me find my way back.
And Jesus—Jesus met me in the ashes.

Now, I walk with faith that does not shake.
Now, I praise not just for blessings—
But even for brokenness.

Because even in sorrow… He is risen.
Even in suffering… He is alive.
And so is my hope.

“We are hard pressed on every side, but not crushed;
Perplexed, but not in despair;
Persecuted, but not abandoned;
Struck down, but not destroyed.
We always carry around in our body the death of Jesus, so that the life of Jesus may also be revealed in us.” — 2 Corinthians 4:8–10 (NIV)

So let it be revealed in me.
In every word.
Every tear.
Every testimony.

I’m still here.
Because Jesus lives in me.
