The Blooming Ache

The sakura is blooming again—
Soft, fragile, and full of life.
But here I am, standing under its petals,
Carrying a heart that's missing someone.

It's strange, how life keeps going
Even when a piece of you
Feels stuck in yesterday—
A moment you can't outgrow.

I smile. I move. I show up.
But inside, there's a silent ache,
Blooming with every reminder—
A memory. A voice. A familiar place.

And suddenly, I’m back to that moment—
When you were here,
And then, you weren’t.
A soft goodbye I never chose.

But maybe that's what love does.
It lingers. It aches. It stays.
And it keeps blooming,
Even when the season has changed.

So I let the ache bloom—
Not to drown in it,
But to honor you,
And the love that still remains.

Because love like that never dies—
It just becomes a softer kind of presence.
A quiet comfort in the petals,
A whispered “I miss you” on the breeze.

"Blessed are those who mourn, for they will be comforted."
— Matthew 5:4
