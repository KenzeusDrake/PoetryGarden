Boundless Grace, Broken Chains

(Inspired by "O Come to the Altar")

Are you tired? Tired of running,
Tired of hiding, tired of pretending you're okay?
Does your soul feel heavy,
Like the weight of everything you've done is too much to carry?

Listen, there's a place where your burdens can rest,
A place where the weight of your past doesn't define your future.
It's not in your own strength,
But in the open arms of grace.

"O come to the altar," He calls,
Not with judgment, but with invitation—
A Father's voice, asking you to come just as you are,
Broken and weary, and let His love make you whole.

The cross wasn't just a sacrifice—
It was a declaration of freedom.
Nailed hands, open arms, saying,
"Come, and lay down your chains."

No matter the scars,
No matter the shame—
There is freedom in His name.
Boundless grace. Broken chains.

These aren't just words; they are a promise—
A promise that your story doesn't end in defeat,
But in redemption.
Why wait?

Why carry the weight
When He already carried it for you?
Why live in fear
When He has already overcome the grave?

The altar is open—
Not a place of condemnation,
But a place of healing.
Come—drop your pain, drop your doubt, and let grace rewrite your story.

Boundless grace. Broken chains.
The invitation is simple:
Come to the altar,
Where love still bleeds for you, where you are made new,
Where your chains fall, and your soul is free.
