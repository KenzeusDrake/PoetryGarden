Before the Night Ends

For My Lolo, With Love and Glory to God

Before the night lays down its head,
Let me whisper words I’ve left unsaid.
Thank you, Lolo—through joy and ache,
For loving me with no heartbreak.

Though I can’t hold your hand today,
Your love has never walked away.
You taught me truth, you taught me grace,
You never left, you held your place.

I miss the times you'd twist my ear,
Whenever I’d be wrong, unclear.
But even that was love so real—
Correction with a heart that feels.

You saw the light inside my flaws,
You cheered me on without applause.
When I had nothing left to show,
You whispered, "Anak, still, you’ll grow."

You never gave up on my soul,
Believed that cracks can still be whole.
You said, "As long as you still breathe,
There’s time to rise from underneath."

And now you rest—no pain, no tears,
But I’ll be holding all our years.
Each lesson, joke, and stern rebuke,
Each tear you wiped, each word you spoke.

And oh, dear God, how deep I thank—
From my first steps to where I sank,
You gave me him—this man so true,
A mirror of Your heart and view.

“Every good and perfect gift is from above,
coming down from the Father of the heavenly lights,
who does not change like shifting shadows.”
(James 1:17)

You didn’t owe me love like that,
But still You gave, and now I’m flat—
On grateful knees, with lifted hands,
To praise the One who understands.

So all my thanks, I raise above—
For Lolo’s life, for Lolo’s love.
Though I still cry, my heart will sing:
All glory to the Risen King.

You loaned me him—a holy grace,
And now You’ve called him to Your place.
So let my sorrow be a song,
That even grief can still belong.

Before the night, before the stars,
I send my prayer past heaven’s bars.
Tell him I’ll miss him, through and through,
But I’ll live out the love he knew.

And one day, Lord, we’ll meet again,
Beyond the trials, beyond the pain.
But while I’m here, I’ll live it true—
To make him proud, and honor You.

All glory to You, my faithful King,
For giving me my everything.
