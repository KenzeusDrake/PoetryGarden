Throttle Therapy: A Moment of Reflection

I remember those days—random, yet clear,
Throttle therapy, a moment of fear.
Rushing, racing, the engine’s roar,
Chasing a high that left me wanting more.

It felt like freedom, but in truth, a cage,
A fleeting escape, yet bound by rage.
The speed, the noise, the endless chase,
I thought it would fill that empty space.

But now, in the stillness, I see the cost—
A part of myself I almost lost.
Chasing that rush, I lost my way,
Forgot who I was, who I am today.

I don’t want to go back to that wasted time,
The life I lived was a mountain to climb.
Each rev of the engine drowned my soul,
I thought I was whole, but never felt whole.

Now, I stand here, grounded and free,
No more chaos to define me.
I remember the throttle, but I chose a different course,
One that heals, one that draws from a greater source.

In my past, there was pain and strife,
But I’ve found peace beyond that life.
Throttle therapy may have brought me near,
But now I know—my true path is clear.

When You Choose Someone to Love

Have you ever paused and asked the skies,
“Lord, is this love or just disguise?”
When someone smiles and makes you glow,
Do you ask Him first, or just go with the flow?

Love is beautiful—yes, it’s true.
But have you prayed, “Is this from You?”
Or do you dive, head over feet,
Without asking if this love is sweet... or sweet deceit?

Before you give your heart away,
Do you kneel down at the end of the day?
Do you whisper, “God, if this is right,
Please give me peace and clear my sight.”

When patience fades and tempers rise,
Will you still love, or just cut ties?
Can you choose someone who stays through pain,
Who’ll dance with you in sun and rain?

Have you asked for someone kind and true—
Not just to you, but also to Him too?
Someone whose hands will help you pray,
Who leads you closer every day?

Because love isn’t just fireworks and thrill—
It’s choosing each other when time stands still.
It’s waiting, trusting, learning grace—
Finding home in someone’s face.

So I’ll ask you now, before you start,
Have you invited God into your heart?
Not just to bless the one you choose—
But to teach you love you’ll never lose?

Stay close to Him—He knows the way.
Let Him write your love story today.
And when the right one comes along, you’ll see:
Love is best when it begins on bended knee.

Life is Just Too Hard

Life is nothing but struggle
And I refuse to believe that
There’s beauty in the broken parts
Because

Hope is a lie we tell ourselves
Joy fades too quickly
And it’s not true that
We grow through what we go through

The pain is permanent
I’m certain
Nothing ever gets better
It’s foolish to think

There’s strength in suffering
I know
Darkness wins in the end
And no one can convince me that

This life is worth it
