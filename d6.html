No Turning Back: Manatiling Liwanag sa Dilim

I once was lost, a soul adrift,
Caught in the wind, no steady lift.
I walked in doubt, I walked in fear,
My faith was small, my heart unclear.

I searched for answers on my own,
Through paths of pain, through seeds once sown.
But all I found were broken dreams,
A heart that cracked and lost its seams.

But then, a light broke through the dark,
A whisper called, igniting spark.
A hand that reached, a voice so kind,
Guided my heart, redefined.

From unbelief, I learned to stand,
A life once lost, now in His hand.
The roots of faith began to grow,
And I was not the one to sow.

For now, my faith is like a tree,
Planted firm in what I can't see.
Its roots run deep beneath the ground,
In the Lord’s love, I’ve found my sound.

No more a soul tossed by the storm,
For now I stand, my heart reborn.
Faith like a mountain, strong and tall,
I will not crumble, I will not fall.

I trust the path, I trust the way,
No matter what the world may say.
For His plans are greater than mine,
I walk by faith, His will divine.

I once was weak, but now I’m strong,
In Him, I find where I belong.
Faith is my fortress, faith is my song,
And with His grace, I will go long.

No turning back, no second guess,
For my faith now is firm, no less.
It’s anchored deep, it’s built to last,
Rooted in truth, unshaken fast.

I stand today, I rise again,
In every storm, through every pain.
My faith is stronger, I won’t sway—
For with Proverbs 3:5-6, I’ll find my way.
