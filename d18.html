POV: “Right Person, Wrong Timing” — Or Was It Just Rushing What Wasn’t Meant Yet?

People say “right person, wrong timing.”
But I’ve been thinking… is it really the timing that’s wrong—or are we just moving ahead of what God prepared?

I’ve never been in a relationship. Never dated anyone.
Not because I didn’t want to, but because I didn’t want to force something I should’ve waited for.
I’ve always believed: if it’s from the Lord, the timing won’t be wrong.

Because what kind of God would place someone “right” in front of you, only to make the season impossible?
That’s not His nature.
God is not the Author of confusion.

So maybe what we call “right person, wrong timing”
was really right feeling, wrong foundation
or right emotion, wrong direction.
Maybe it was a rushed prayer, not a revealed promise.

I know that feeling too—
Not in love, but in calling.

I remember when I got offered something that looked like an answered prayer. A job. A role. Something I wanted for a long time.
And part of me whispered, “This is it.”
But something deeper said, “You’re not ready yet.”

I didn’t ignore it.
I turned it down.
And it hurt—because it felt so right.

But if I’m being honest… maybe it only looked right, because I was tired of waiting.
And I realized: it’s not always the opportunity that’s off—it’s sometimes our urgency to prove we’re ready.

Isaiah 60:22 (NIV):
“When the time is right, I, the Lord, will make it happen.”

And that one verse?
That’s my anchor.
That’s how I measure timing now.

If it requires me to force, manipulate, beg, or compromise—then it’s not time.
If it gives more anxiety than peace, more pressure than purpose—then it’s not God’s moment.

I believe in God’s timing so much that I’d rather lose ten “almosts” than rush one thing that wasn’t fully mine.
Because when the Lord writes it—whether it’s a person, a job, or a calling—it comes with clarity, not confusion.

Ecclesiastes 3:11 (NIV):
“He has made everything beautiful in its time.”
Not my time. Not when I feel ready. But when He says it’s time.

So now when I hear “right person, wrong timing,”
I ask:
Was it the wrong timing?
Or was it the wrong surrender?

Because if it’s God who sends them, you won’t need to fight time.
Time will open for you.
And love—real love—will come not when you feel ready,
But when God has readied both of you.
