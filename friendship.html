Not by Blood, But by Heart

What if I never answered back?
What if I kept scrolling past—
Kept him as just another name,
Another face that wouldn't last?

What if that second message
Was never met with mine?
Would I have missed a brother
I was meant to find in time?

The first time, I didn’t care.
Didn’t think twice, didn’t look deep.
But he came again—with words so simple,
And something in me took the leap.

One reply. That’s all it took.
One choice to write instead of stay silent—
And a whole new chapter opened
Like a book, warm and vibrant.

He wasn’t born into my story.
We share no blood, no family tree.
But somehow, this stranger
Became home to a part of me.

He listens like a brother would.
He speaks like a friend who knows.
And in the quiet moments,
His loyalty always shows.

We’ve never shared a room,
Or walked the same street lane—
But somehow, he carries my burdens
And helps me through my pain.

So if you ask how this all began,
I’ll say—“By chance… but it stayed by choice.”
Because bonds like this aren’t planned—
They’re felt beyond the noise.

He is not my brother by birth—
Not by blood, not by name…
But he’s my brother just the same,
And I’d never trade that claim.

And in the heart, that’s where it starts.
A love that distance can’t tear apart.
He’s proof that family can form apart—
Not by blood. But by heart.
